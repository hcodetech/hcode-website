# Travel Industry Transformation: The Importance of Customer-Centric Booking Platforms

![Travel booking app in mobile](/assets/blog/2024/07/view-hands-holding-smartphone.jpg)

Online travel platforms have completely changed how people plan, research, and book their travel, with a growing preference for mobile bookings, especially among younger generations. Nearly 40% of travelers look for platforms that provide a connected trip experience that seamlessly integrates various aspects of a journey, including transportation, accommodations, activities, and services.

Amidst this demographic shift in the travel industry, customer-centric booking platforms hold the key to unlocking future potential. A unified travel experience that encompasses route planning, booking assistance, travel bookings, and a Semitic illustration will be the travel platforms in the near future.  

To accommodate these rapid changes in the dynamic travel industry, let us establish the need for a customer-centric experience in the present landscape and find out what you, as a decision-maker, can do to leap first and tap the potential of travel digitalization. 

## Travel APP Features for Customer-Centric Experience

Online Travel Agency (OTA) is already experimenting with AI-powered chatbots, mainly focusing on tools that suggest trip itineraries and facilitate bookings. This seems to target the vast majority of young audiences that prefer a Google search to talking to travel advisors before a trip. 

Young people are the most frequent travelers accounting for 20% of all international visitors internationally. 81% of these young users reported using online travel tips and resources, highlighting the importance of digital assistance in their travel decisions.

Most travelers prefer these five features when in need of travel assistance.

### Route detector

Google Maps is integral for travelers, providing directions for over 20 billion kilometers daily as of 2023, showcasing its extensive application in travel and logistics. Whenever visiting a new destination, maps become the only choice for travelers.

Seeing this interaction with maps, online travel planning apps are integrating a personalized map feature that directs the best route according to your itinerary. After planning your trip with the application you can explore the best options available to travel, with a real-time guiding feature, making it a lot more convenient than switching destinations in Google Maps. 

This has shown growing engagement time among applications that adopted this functionality improving customer satisfaction and retention on the platform.

### AR-VR

After the pandemic users entered in a habit to explore the destination beforehand. If it is from visitor ratings, images, or videos, these add readability to the destination and provide the travel with confidence. Thus, travel apps have long worked to enhance the first impression of places using advanced AI algorithms to select the best-representing images and videos of destinations. 

Recent breakthroughs in AR and VR have also influenced this segment of the travel experience. The application now integrates a 3D visualization of your destination to enhance the customer experience. Such platforms with AR-VR features have also seen a surge of customers interacting with these new features. Although the technology is new and not widely accepted, the future of the travel industry shows promise for AR-VR mainstreaming in travel app user interactions. 

### Comparison Options

Nowadays, where the travel industry is so competitive offering users detailed comparison options is a bit more important. For instance, the traditional means of comparing prices, facilities, and user ratings in various service providers are now available as a feature set on travel apps.

With these features in place, travelers would be able to make well-informed decisions and expect the best offers for their money. These comparison tools are also augmented by filters and sorting options, which enhances the decision-making process for more efficient travel planning.

### Customer-Centric Features

The customer is the heart of any service, and in today’s travel applications world, consumer-centric has become a new dictum. Various different modern travel apps place an emphasis on personalization to improve the overall user experience.

These apps algorithmically generate recommendations based on user data, historical behavior, and real-time information using the power of data analytics & machine learning. Personalized itineraries, customized offers, and loyalty programs specifically meet the requirements of each user resulting in an engaging travel planning experience that increases satisfaction.

### Virtual Assistants

Virtual assistants are changing the way users work with travel apps. This conversational AI enables instant help, answers to questions, booking holidays, and travel recommendations.

Travel apps take user experience to the next level by incorporating virtual assistants that ensure a smooth operation process of managing travel plans. Having 24/7 access to a virtual assistant drastically enhances the traveler user experience which ultimately means higher levels of customer satisfaction and engagement compared to any other travel industry.

## Factors Affecting Customer Experience in a Travel APP

### Personalized Experience

Personalization has become essential in the travel industry. Research by Salesforce reveals that 84% of customers prioritize being treated as individuals rather than numbers when making business decisions. Modern booking platforms utilize data analytics and machine learning to customize recommendations based on a traveler’s previous actions, preferences, and search history. For instance, Booking.com employs AI to analyze user data and provide personalized travel options, ensuring travelers find choices that align with their preferences.

### Seamless User Interface

A well-designed user interface is crucial for enhancing the booking experience. Forrester’s research indicates that an effective user interface can boost conversion rates by up to 200%. Customer-centric platforms focus on intuitive navigation and clear information, making it easy for users to search, compare, and book their travel plans. Features such as interactive maps, virtual tours, and detailed filters support the decision-making process. Airbnb’s interface, for example, is celebrated for its simplicity, allowing users to book accommodations with ease.

### Integrated Services

Today’s travelers value convenience and integrated services are a significant appeal of modern booking platforms. These platforms offer more than just flights and accommodations; they also include services like car rentals, travel insurance, and local activities. This comprehensive approach simplifies trip planning, enabling travelers to manage all aspects of their journey in one place. For example, Expedia offers a wide range of services, from flights and hotels to car rentals and activities, enhancing user convenience and satisfaction.

### 24/7 Customer Support

Reliable customer support is vital due to the unpredictable nature of travel plans. Customer-centric booking platforms provide round-the-clock support via live chat, email, and phone, ensuring travelers receive help whenever needed. A Microsoft study found that 90% of consumers expect an immediate response from customer service. Platforms like TripAdvisor offer 24/7 support to promptly address customer inquiries and issues.

### Mobile Accessibility

In the age of smartphones, mobile accessibility is a critical feature. According to eMarketer, mobile travel bookings accounted for 48% of digital travel sales in 2020. Customer-centric platforms offer responsive designs and mobile apps, allowing travelers to book and manage trips on the go. Mobile notifications and updates keep users informed about their bookings and any changes. The Kayak app, for instance, provides real-time flight status updates, price alerts, and itinerary management, simplifying travel planning.

### Customer Reviews and Ratings

Customer reviews and ratings significantly influence the decision-making process. A BrightLocal survey found that 91% of consumers read online reviews before making a purchase. Customer-centric platforms showcase verified reviews and ratings from fellow travelers, offering valuable insights into accommodations, attractions, and services. This transparency helps users make informed choices and builds confidence in the platform. For example, TripAdvisor’s extensive review system enables travelers to read detailed feedback from other users, aiding their travel planning.

### Security and Trust

Security is paramount for travelers, particularly when conducting online transactions. Customer-centric booking platforms invest in robust security measures, such as encryption and secure payment gateways, to protect user data and financial information. Ensuring security and reliability is crucial for retaining customers and fostering trust. A PwC study indicates that 85% of consumers avoid businesses with questionable security practices. Platforms like Expedia use advanced encryption technologies to safeguard user data.

### Sustainable Travel Options

With increasing environmental awareness, many travelers seek sustainable travel options. Booking.com’s 2021 Sustainable Travel Report shows that 83% of global travelers consider sustainable travel important. Customer-centric platforms address this demand by promoting eco-friendly accommodations, sustainable tours, and carbon-offset programs. Highlighting sustainable choices aligns with travelers’ values and enhances the brand’s image. EcoHotels.com, for instance, specializes in offering environmentally friendly lodging options.

### Loyalty Programs and Incentives

Loyalty programs and incentives are effective for retaining customers and encouraging repeat bookings. Customer-centric booking platforms provide rewards programs, exclusive discounts, and personalized deals to loyal customers. These incentives boost customer satisfaction and foster long-term relationships. Bond’s Loyalty Report indicates that 79% of consumers are more likely to continue doing business with brands that offer a loyalty program. Platforms like Hotels.com offer a rewards program where users can earn free nights after a certain number of bookings.
