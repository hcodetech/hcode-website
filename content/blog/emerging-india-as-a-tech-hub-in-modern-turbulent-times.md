###### EMERGING INDIA AS A TECH HUB IN MODERN TURBULENT TIMES

![](/assets/blog/2022/12/Group-1-2.png)

The post-Covid era has been full of ups and downs, and projections clouding us to see a clear future. Amid the difficult situation as offshoring increased in the IT sector, India has become a rising tech star. How?

* * *

#### **The cadence of the Post-Covid era**

We have all changed how we operate during the post-Covid-19 crisis. Some changes got forced on us; others represented the height of innovation in a difficult situation. There has been a [reset of the workforce](https://www.gartner.com/smarterwithgartner/reset-your-business-strategy-in-covid-19-recovery#:~:text=There%E2%80%99s%20been%20a%20reset%20of%20the%20workforce%20and%20work%20itself%2C%20a%20reset%20of%20the%20employer/employee%20relationship%20and%20a%20reset%20of%20the%20business%20ecosystem.%20For%20most%2C%20the%20business%20impact%20of%20the%20pandemic%20has%20been%20negative%3B%20for%20some%2C%20positive.%C2%A0), employer/employee relationship, and the business ecosystem. For [some companies](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/mckinsey-explainers/what-is-a-recession#:~:text=Companies%20head%20into%20periods%20of%20uncertainty%E2%80%94like%20the%20unprecedented%20post%2DCOVID%2D19%20economic%20climate%E2%80%94with%20varying%20degrees%20of%20readiness%20and%20health.%20Most%20fall%20into%20one%20of%20four%20camps%3A), the impact of the pandemic has been positive; for some, apocalypse.

The phases of the Covid-19 pandemic are still in progress. In general, today’s environment is economically ambiguous.

1.  The record inflation in the United States ([inflation rate of 7.1%](https://www.statista.com/statistics/273418/unadjusted-monthly-inflation-rate-in-the-us/) in Dec 2022 from 2.5% in Jan 2020).
2.  The [government’s fiscal responses](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/strategy-and-corporate-finance/our-insights/global-economics-intelligence-executive-summary-september-2022#:~:text=This%20battle%20against,and%20the%20euro.) contribute to recession concerns, but the credit and term spread signal lower risk.
3.  Unemployment remains at the [multidecade lows](https://www.gartner.com/en/articles/supply-chain-leaders-its-time-to-rethink-your-recession-playbook#:~:text=Unemployment%20is%20likely%20to%20rise%2C%20according%20to%20some%20models%2C%20but%20for%20now%20remains%20at%20multidecade%20lows.) and is likely to rise.
4.  And then, global geopolitical instability after Russia invades Ukraine.

Now, the [question for the CEOs](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/strategy-and-corporate-finance/our-insights/navigating-inflation-a-new-playbook-for-ceos#:~:text=Where%20will%20customers,all%20this%20activity%3F) is how to combat this period for a better today and future.

* * *

#### **Offshoring of IT and the rise of India**

Accelerating the right digital initiatives lies at the core of must-take actions in today’s economy. [Lessons learned](https://www.gartner.com/smarterwithgartner/act-now-to-fund-innovation-and-growth) from prior downturns show that industry leaders accelerate in economic downturns by boldly investing in digital initiatives. According to the [latest forecast by Gartner, Inc.](https://www.gartner.com/en/newsroom/press-releases/2022-10-19-gartner-forecasts-worldwide-it-spending-to-grow-5-percent-in-2023), worldwide IT spending is projected to total $4.6 trillion in 2023, an increase of 5.1% from 2022.

Companies are [opting to augment](https://www.gartner.com/en/articles/9-winning-actions-to-take-as-recession-threatens#:~:text=Augment%20and%20automate%20activities%20with%20technology%20such%20as%20artificial%20intelligence%20or%20robotics%20to%20reduce%20labor%20costs%2C%20shore%20up%20production%20and%20free%20up%20scarce%2C%20high%2Dcost%20talent%20to%20focus%20on%20value%2Dcreating%20activities.) their tech teams with offshore talent to tackle the squeeze of persistently high inflation and limit expensive talent. Successful offshoring requires a stable, low-risk environment that enables a business to operate seamlessly.

A key recipient of a large inflow of offshoring work has been **India**. Encouraged by attractive [fiscal incentives](https://www.makeinindia.com/eodb), an investment-friendly environment, and lower living costs, overseas companies are [embracing offshoring strategies](https://www.persolkelly.com/news/india-builds-business-agility-with-offshoring-and-contingent-workforce-strategies/59492/#:~:text=India%20Builds%20Business%20Agility%20with%20Offshoring%20and%20Contingent%20Workforce%20Strategies) and transferring activities of specific business processes to India, particularly IT.

As per the [growth forecast by the IMF](https://www.imf.org/en/Publications/WEO/Issues/2022/07/26/world-economic-outlook-update-july-2022), India will remain one of the fastest growing economies globally for the financial year 2022-23, where GDP growth projections currently stand at 7.4% – being more than twice the rate forecast for China (3.3%) and three times higher than the United States (2.3%). India’s exports of [software services climbed 17.2% in the 2021-22 period](https://rbidocs.rbi.org.in/rdocs/PressRelease/PDFs/PR840F6A458180D874C4C8CEF9A5C3B64133F.PDF), reaching an estimated total of US$156.7 billion.

#### **India for the future of tech work**

India has all the [right ingredients](https://www.persolkelly.com/news/india-has-all-the-right-ingredients-for-building-a-future-ready-workforce/53977/#:~:text=INDIA%20has%20all%20the%20right%20ingredients%20for%20building%20a%20future%2Dready%20workforce) for building a future-ready tech workforce.

**STEM-driven education system:** As per UNESCO data, nearly one-third of students in India pick STEM subjects, resulting in [more STEM graduates than any other country bar China](https://www.statista.com/chart/22927/share-and-total-number-of-stem-graduates-by-country/).

**Highly skilled and qualified manpower:** India generates 1 million graduates annually and the Indian infrastructure includes 3500 engineering colleges, 3400 polytechnics, and 200 schools of planning and architecture.

**Changing tech landscape:** India’s Technology landscape is evolving rapidly from Tier-1 cities to [Tier-2, Tier-3, and Tier-4 cities](https://yourstory.com/2022/11/how-tier-2-3-and-4-cities-are-contributing-to-indian-startup-boom) to target new opportunities.

**Time zone variations:** Companies in India provide the quickest time-to-market. For example- the 12-hour time difference between India and the USA leads to the faster completion of work, which further leads to increased productivity with greater efficiency.

**Language barriers:** India is the leading country of English speakers in Asia with [265 million who can speak the language](https://worldpopulationreview.com/country-rankings/english-speaking-countries#:~:text=This%20continent%20has%20about%20460%20million%20people%20who%20can%20speak%20English.%20India%20leads%20as%20the%20country%20with%20a%20high%20number%20of%20people%20who%20can%20speak%20English%20at%20265%20million).  Due to the skills, communication, technology, deliverables, and many more advantages, India has become a global destination for outsourcing IT services.

**Capacitive corporate environment:** Indian government’s push towards digitalization and favorable policies and tax structures has helped overseas companies to take advantage of Indian talent and infrastructure.
